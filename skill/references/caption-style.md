# Caption Style

Use these rules when preparing transcript text before generating SRT.

## Thai

- Use natural spoken Thai.
- Keep particles such as "ครับ", "ค่ะ", "นะ", "เลย", and "มาก" with the phrase they modify.
- Avoid overly formal wording unless the source is formal.
- Preserve names, products, numbers, prices, and claims exactly.

## English

- Use concise conversational English.
- Translate meaning, not word order.
- Keep the English line shorter than the Thai line when possible.
- Avoid adding marketing claims or extra emphasis not present in the source.

## Bilingual Layout

- Prefer two-line cues: Thai on line 1, English on line 2.
- If either language is long, split into separate cue pairs rather than making four-line captions.
- Keep related Thai and English lines in the same cue.
- Keep punctuation simple and readable.

## Timing

- Without real audio timestamps, distribute cue timing by text length.
- Give each cue enough time to read; 1.1 seconds is a practical minimum for short cues.
- Leave no gaps unless the user asks for pauses.
